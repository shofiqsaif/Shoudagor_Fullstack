### Key Purchase Order Reports for Distributorship Businesses

In a distributorship business, where purchasing goods from suppliers and distributing them to retailers or customers is central, purchase order (PO) reports provide critical insights into supply chain efficiency, cost management, supplier performance, and cash flow. These reports help prevent stockouts, negotiate better terms, identify delays, and optimize inventory turnover. Based on established systems like Stampli, Infor Distribution FACTS, Microsoft Dynamics GP, Deltek Vision, and Extensiv, here are some of the most impactful PO reports used in real businesses. I've included descriptions, examples, and their specific benefits, focusing on distributorship contexts. Where applicable, I've also suggested novel adaptations that build on these standards for added impact.

#### 1. Purchase Order Status/Tracking Report

* **Description** : This report provides a real-time overview of all POs, showing their progress from creation to delivery, including status (e.g., draft, approved, shipped, received), dates, vendor details, amounts, and expected delivery. It often allows filtering by vendor, project, or cost category.
* **Example** : In a clothing distributorship, the report might show a PO for 5,000 units of T-shirts from Vendor A as "shipped" with an expected delivery in 3 days, while another for accessories from Vendor B is "on hold" due to approval delays.
* **Impact/Improvement** : It enables proactive monitoring to resolve delays quickly, preventing production or distribution halts. For distributorships, this reduces stockouts during peak seasons, improves on-time delivery rates (potentially by 20-30% with automation), and enhances customer satisfaction by ensuring reliable inventory availability. Businesses like those using Stampli have used it to avoid operational disruptions from missing components.

#### 2. Open or Created Purchase Orders Report

* **Description** : Lists all active or recently created POs that are open (approved but not fully received or paid), partially open, or closed within a date range. Includes metrics like total value, count of POs, average cost, and status summaries.
* **Example** : A food distributorship reviews a report showing 15 open POs totaling $150,000 for perishable goods, with 5 partially received, highlighting trends in frequent orders from a key supplier.
* **Impact/Improvement** : Tracks spending against budgets and identifies bottlenecks like excessive open POs, which could signal supplier issues. In distributorships, it optimizes inventory planning by forecasting incoming stock, reduces overstocking costs (saving 10-15% on holding expenses), and supports demand anticipation during seasonal fluctuations, as seen in systems like Deltek Vision where it analyzes overdue items.

#### 3. Vendor Performance Report

* **Description** : Analyzes spending and performance by vendor, including POs issued, total values, open/closed counts, delivery timeliness, and quality metrics. Often includes top vendors by cost or volume.
* **Example** : An electronics distributorship's report reveals 80% of POs go to one supplier with a 95% on-time delivery rate, while another has only 70% and frequent quality rejections.
* **Impact/Improvement** : Facilitates better negotiation for discounts or terms and mitigates risks from over-reliance on single vendors. For distributorships, it improves supply chain resilience by diversifying suppliers, potentially cutting costs by 5-10% through bulk deals, and reduces disruptions—real-world examples from Stampli show businesses diversifying to ensure steady supply during shortages.

#### 4. PO Aging Report

* **Description** : Categorizes POs by age (e.g., 0-30 days, 31-60 days, 90+ days), showing details like vendor, creation date, status, and expected delivery, with summaries of counts and values per bucket.
* **Example** : In a pharmaceutical distributorship, the report flags 10 POs over 60 days old from a slow supplier, totaling $50,000, prompting escalation for expedited delivery.
* **Impact/Improvement** : Highlights delays and evaluates vendor reliability, optimizing cash flow by predicting upcoming payments. Distributorships benefit by minimizing aged POs that tie up capital, improving working capital turnover (up to 15-20%), and preventing inventory gaps that could lead to lost sales, as demonstrated in Stampli case studies where it streamlined supplier processes.

#### 5. Purchase Order Progress Report

* **Description** : Tracks the fulfillment status of individual POs, including quantities ordered, received, remaining, expected dates, and any variances.
* **Example** : A beverage distributorship views a report for a PO of 10,000 cases of soda, showing 6,000 received, 4,000 remaining, with an expected completion in 5 days.
* **Impact/Improvement** : Provides visibility into partial fulfillments, helping adjust inventory forecasts. In distributorships, it enhances efficiency by reducing backorders and enabling just-in-time stocking, potentially lowering storage costs by 10% and improving order fulfillment rates, as used in Extensiv for warehouse operations.

#### 6. Received/Not Invoiced Report (or Uninvoiced Receipts Report)

* **Description** : Compares received goods against invoiced ones, listing POs with discrepancies, including quantities received but not billed, vendors, and dates.
* **Example** : A hardware distributorship identifies $20,000 in received tools from a vendor that haven't been invoiced, allowing for accrual adjustments before month-end.
* **Impact/Improvement** : Ensures accurate financial reporting and prevents overpayments or surprises. For distributorships, it supports better cash flow management by anticipating bills, reduces accounting errors (improving accuracy by 20-30%), and maintains strong vendor relations through timely reconciliations, as in Dynamics GP and Infor systems.

#### 7. Cost Variance Report

* **Description** : Details differences between expected PO costs and actual invoiced or received costs, including variances by line item, vendor, or period.
* **Example** : An automotive parts distributorship spots a 5% cost overrun on a PO for tires due to price fluctuations, triggering a review of contract terms.
* **Impact/Improvement** : Identifies pricing issues early for corrective action. In distributorships, it controls margins by flagging unfavorable variances, potentially saving 5-15% on procurement costs through renegotiations, and enhances profitability analysis, as utilized in Dynamics GP for auditing.

#### 8. Cash Requirements Report (Novel Adaptation: Supplier Cash Flow Forecast Report)

* **Description** : Forecasts future payments based on open POs, including due dates, amounts, and vendors. A novel extension could integrate predictive analytics to forecast based on historical PO trends and market data.
* **Example** : A consumer goods distributorship projects $100,000 in payments due in the next 30 days from 20 POs, adjusting for seasonal spikes in demand.
* **Impact/Improvement** : Aids in liquidity planning and avoids cash shortages. For distributorships, this novel forecast version could predict supply disruptions, improving budgeting accuracy by 15-25% and enabling early financing arrangements, building on Infor's standard report for more proactive supply chain management.

These reports, drawn from widely used ERP and AP systems, can be customized in tools like Stampli or Dynamics GP to fit distributorship needs. Implementing them often leads to 10-20% efficiency gains in procurement, as evidenced by real business applications. If integrated with analytics dashboards, they further amplify insights for strategic decision-making.
